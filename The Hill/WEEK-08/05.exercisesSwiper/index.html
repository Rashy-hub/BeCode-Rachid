Daenerys Targaryen

House Targaryen

Daenerys Targaryen, the last surviving child of King Aerys II Targaryen, is known as the Mother of Dragons. Forced into exile after her family's downfall, she grows from a meek, oppressed girl into a powerful and determined leader. Driven by a vision of reclaiming the Iron Throne and ending injustice, Daenerys commands a vast following and wields three dragons, the first seen in centuries, as she strives to conquer and liberate.

Sansa Stark

House Stark

Sansa Stark, the eldest daughter of Eddard and Catelyn Stark, begins the series as a naive and romantic girl dreaming of becoming a queen. Her experiences in King's Landing, including her forced marriage to Tyrion Lannister and subsequent capture by Ramsay Bolton, harden her and teach her the brutal realities of politics and power. Sansa evolves into a shrewd and resilient leader, ultimately reclaiming her home and title as Lady of Winterfell.

Arya Stark

House Stark

Arya Stark, the youngest daughter of Eddard and Catelyn Stark, is fiercely independent and rebellious. Unlike her sister Sansa, Arya rejects traditional gender roles and aspires to be a warrior. Her journey is marked by a relentless quest for vengeance against those who wronged her family. Arya's adventures take her across Westeros and beyond, where she trains with the Faceless Men, becoming a skilled assassin with a list of names to cross off.

Jon Snow

House Stark

Jon Snow, born Aegon Targaryen, is the son of Lyanna Stark and Rhaegar Targaryen, the late Prince of Dragonstone. From infancy, Jon is presented as the bastard son of Lord Eddard Stark, Lyanna's brother, and raised alongside Eddard's lawful children at Winterfell. Jon's true parentage is kept secret from everyone, including Jon himself, in order to protect him from those that sought the complete annihilation of House Targaryen.

Eddard Stark

House Stark

Eddard "Ned" Stark is the head of House Stark, Warden of the North, and Lord of Winterfell. He is a man of honor and integrity, deeply committed to the well-being of his family and the people of the North. Ned is a close friend of King Robert Baratheon, who appoints him as Hand of the King. His sense of duty and justice often puts him at odds with the treacherous politics of King's Landing, ultimately leading to his downfall.

Tyrion Lannister

House Lannister

Tyrion Lannister, the youngest son of Tywin Lannister, is a dwarf often mocked and underestimated by others. Despite this, he is one of the most intelligent and resourceful characters in Westeros. Known for his wit and sharp tongue, Tyrion navigates the dangerous political landscape with cunning and strategy. He seeks recognition and respect, often finding himself at odds with his powerful family, particularly his father and sister.

Joffrey Baratheon

House Baratheon

Joffrey Baratheon, ostensibly the son of King Robert Baratheon and Queen Cersei Lannister, is actually the product of Cersei's incestuous relationship with her brother Jaime. As king, Joffrey is cruel, sadistic, and capricious, relishing in the suffering of others. His tyrannical rule and unpredictable behavior make him one of the most hated characters in Westeros, eventually leading to his violent and much-celebrated demise.

Jaime Lannister

House Lannister

Jaime Lannister, known as the Kingslayer, is the eldest son of Tywin Lannister and a member of the Kingsguard. Renowned for his swordsmanship, Jaime is both admired and reviled for killing King Aerys II Targaryen during Robert's Rebellion. Despite his outward arrogance and reckless behavior, Jaime's character is complex, driven by a deep love for his sister Cersei and a desire to redeem himself in the eyes of others.

Cersei Lannister

House Lannister

Cersei Lannister is the only daughter of Tywin Lannister and the twin sister of Jaime Lannister. As Queen, she is fiercely protective of her children and ruthless in her pursuit of power. Cersei's manipulative and ambitious nature drives much of the conflict in the series, as she uses her cunning and influence to eliminate her enemies and secure her family's dominance. Her deep-seated hatred for the Stark family and her willingness to do anything for her children define her character and actions throughout the series.
